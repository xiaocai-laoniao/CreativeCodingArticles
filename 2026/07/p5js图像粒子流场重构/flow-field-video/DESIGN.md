# Flow Field Explainer Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

A cinematic dark technical explainer that inherits the article page language: cold blue particle traces, warm amber indexing, restrained panels, and soft depth. The video should feel like an algorithm being dissected in a studio, not a generic SaaS presentation. The main emotional beat is "a static image becomes a motion map."

## Colors

- Background: `#08090d` deep blue-black canvas.
- Panel: `#111318` dark graphite cards and browser frames.
- Text: `#f3f0ea` warm off-white primary copy.
- Muted Text: `#a9b0bd` secondary explanation.
- Flow Cyan: `#8bdcff` vectors, particle lines, data highlights.
- Warm Amber: `#d7a86e` step numbers, thesis accents, scan markers.
- Steel Blue: `#51657d` low-contrast grid and structural rules.

## Typography

- Display: `"Songti SC", "STSong", serif` for large Chinese headlines with editorial weight.
- Body: `"PingFang SC", "Hiragino Sans GB", "Microsoft YaHei", sans-serif` for readable Chinese explanation.
- Data: `"JetBrains Mono", "SF Mono", monospace` for formulas, labels, and timing metadata.

## Motion Rules

- Primary transition: soft push / blur between related scenes.
- Accent transition: quick dark wipe for topic shifts.
- Entrances should be staggered and directional, never all from the same axis.
- Keep ambient particle lines drifting slowly behind the content.
- Use final fade only on the last scene.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use purple-blue gradient hero styling.
- Do not center every scene as one floating text block.
- Do not use jump cuts between scenes.
- Do not use emoji, placeholder icons, or decorative fake UI.
- Do not make the explanation feel like a marketing landing page.
